B.J. Habibie

Prof. Dr.-Ing. Ir. H. Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie, FREng.
Indonesian engineer and the third president of Indonesia.

Here's a time line of B.J. Habibie's life

• 1936 - Born in Parepare, Indonesia.

• 1954 - He studied at Bandung Institute of Technology and continue his study at Delft University of Technology in the Netherlands to study aviation and aerospace. Due to political reasons, he had to continue his study at RWTH Aachen University in Germany.

• 1960 - He received an engineer's degree in Germany with the title Diplom-Ingenieur.

• 1962 - He returned to Indonesia for three months on sick leave. During this time, he was reacquainted with Hasri Ainun.

• 1965 - Habibie delivered his dissertation in aerospace engineering and received the grade of "very good," giving him the title Doktoringenieur (Dr.-Ing.).

• 1974 - Suharto (the second president of Indonesia) recruited Habibie to return to Indonesia as part of his drive to industrialize and develop the country.

• 1978 - Habibie was appointed as Indonesia State Minister of Research and Technology until 1998.

• March 1998 - He was elected as Indonesia vice president.

• May 1998 - Just two months into Habibie's vice presidency, Suharto announced his resignation. Habibie, as the Constitution says, succeeded him as president. He served as the countryâ€™s president until October 1999.

• September 2019 - Dies at the age of 83.
